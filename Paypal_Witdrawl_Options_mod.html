This summary focuses on the only viable, regulated method for an individual to access $100 USD received on PayPal using the FNB integration.

PayPal Withdrawal Options (FNB Integrated Holder)

For a private individual with a linked FNB-PayPal account, there is only one option to access the funds, which involves a mandatory currency conversion.

1. Withdrawal Option: Credit to Main FNB Account

Feature Detail
Process Mandatory Conversion to ZAR. The user logs into their FNB Online Banking or App, initiates the "Withdraw from PayPal" service, enters the $100 amount, and FNB converts the USD to ZAR (after all fees and at their retail exchange rate). The ZAR amount is then deposited.
Transaction Type Credit (Deposit) to the linked FNB (or non-FNB) checking/current account.
Cash/ATM Access Indirect. Once the funds are credited to the FNB account in ZAR, the money becomes normal South African Rand, and the user can then withdraw it as cash from any ATM or FNB branch, subject to daily limits.
Processing Time Instant. For FNB account holders, the ZAR funds are credited to the main FNB account balance instantly after confirming the transaction.

2. No Direct Cash or USD Withdrawal

  • No Direct ATM Withdrawal: You cannot use an ATM or FNB branch to withdraw $100 USD directly from the PayPal balance. The withdrawal must first be converted and credited to a bank account.
  • No Direct USD Retention (Individuals): You cannot withdraw the $100 USD and keep it in a foreign currency account (FCA) like an FNB Global Account as an individual. This option is generally restricted to FNB Business Global (CFC) accounts with a high minimum withdrawal amount (e.g., $5,000).

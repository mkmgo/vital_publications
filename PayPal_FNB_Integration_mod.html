The integration process allows South African PayPal users (both FNB and non-FNB clients) to withdraw funds to any local bank account in compliance with SARB (South African Reserve Bank) regulations.

1. PayPal Account Requirements

  • Verified PayPal Account: Your PayPal account must be fully verified.
  • Verification Method: This is typically done by linking a Debit or Credit Card to PayPal. PayPal makes a small charge containing a 4-digit code in the transaction reference on your bank statement. You must log back into PayPal to enter this code to complete verification.
  • Linked Bank Account: You do not need a bank account linked to PayPal directly; the withdrawal service handles the link through FNB.

2. FNB Account Requirements

  • FNB Online Banking Profile: You must register for a free FNB Online Banking Profile on the FNB website or FNB App. This is required even if you bank with another institution (e.g., Standard Bank, Capitec, etc.).
  • Local Bank Account: You need a valid, ZAR-denominated South African bank account (FNB or non-FNB) to receive the withdrawal funds.

3. Integration Process (Linking)

  1. Log in to FNB: Log into your FNB Online Banking profile or the FNB App.
  2. Navigate to Forex: Go to the Forex or PayPal Services section.
  3. Link Profile: Select the option to Link PayPal Profile.
  4. PayPal Authentication: You will be redirected to the PayPal website to securely log in and grant FNB permission to access your profile for withdrawal purposes.
  5. Confirm Details: You will confirm the local bank account (FNB or non-FNB) you want to use for withdrawals.

4. Important Tips

Tip Detail
SARB Mandate All transactions are automatically reported to the SARB, and funds must generally be withdrawn to a South African account within 30 days of receipt.
Troubleshooting If you encounter a linking loop or error, contact the FNB Forex Advisory team directly, as branch staff may not have the specific authority to resolve system errors. FNB Forex Contact: 0860 1 FOREX (36739) or email forex@fnb.co.za.
Linking Channel While the FNB App is popular, some users find the initial linking process on the desktop FNB Online Banking website more stable.
